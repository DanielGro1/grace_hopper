Grace Hopper

Grace Hopper (1906‚Äì1992) was a computer scientist, United States Navy rear admiral, and one of the earliest computer programmers. She was known for her pioneering work in computer programming and her role in the development of the first compiler for a computer programming language.

  • Developed the first compiler for computer programming languages.
  • Helped develop COBOL, one of the earliest high-level programming languages.
  • Popularized the term "debugging" in reference to fixing computer bugs.
